Patent Distribution Across US Metropolitan Areas

An Analysis of Patent Counts Trends from 2000 to 2015

1. Geographic Distribution of Patent Activity

The above visualization shows the patent counts across U.S. Metropoliton Statistical Areas (MSAs) for a selected year using a chloropeth map. Each MSA is shaded according to its patent count, where darker colors represent higher values and lighter colours represent lower values.

A dropdown menu at the top left allows to swtich between years from 2000 to 2015. When a year is selected, the shading pattern of map changes according to the patent count values of that year.

Key Observation: The map has red markers for the top 5 MSAs having the highet patent counts for the seletced year. The top 5 are San Jose, San Francisco, NYC, Boston and Los Angeles. The top 5 remains similar across the years.

The west coast and east coast show the highest number of patents. In the middle, Chicago and Detroit is showing high number of patent counts. Overall, the patents are increased nationwide with a subtotal of 80,000 in 2000 to 135,000 in 2015.

2. Regional Changes in Patent Activity (2000-2015)

The above visualization shows the distribution of delta percentage change in patent activity across U.S. metropolitan statistical areas from 2000 to 2015. In contrast to the first map, this second choropleth map displays change in number of patents in terms of percentage where green areas represent positive growth, while red areas show declines.

This map shows changes occur which vary across regions. A category filter is a dropdown which allows to see MSAs within specific growth ranges.

Key Observation: The map shows that Seattle, San Jose, Sacramento, Salt Lake City, regions around Colorado and Denver have strong growth in the number of patents count. Some areas e.g. New York has a huge number of patent counts but showed a moderate growth rate. The darkest red decline MSAs can be seen in Rochester, Buffalo and in the Midwest it can be seen in Detroit and Ohio.

Overall, it can be seen that most MSAs fall in the moderate growth rate category with some having highest growth rate while some have seen strongest decline. Some also fall under single digit percentage change in growth.

The combination of both the visualization gives a broader picture of regional patent trends across the Metropolitan Statistical Areas of United States.

Data Source: United States Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO)

Geographic Data: US Census Bureau Metropolitan Statistical Areas

Analysis Period: 2000-2015
